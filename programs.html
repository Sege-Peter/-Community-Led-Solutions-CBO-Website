Our Theory of Change

Core Belief

If we transform the consciousness, attitudes, behaviors, and skills of community members, we will create a critical mass of resourceful people who will effectively offer solutions to their challenges.

Methods

  • Capacity building & personal transformation workshops
  • Community dialogues
  • Creating linkages and networks
  • Implementing community projects
  • Raising voices to demand rights

How CLS CBO Works

  1. Facilitating Community Engagement: Listen, foster participation, and encourage dialogue.
  2. Building Community Capacity: Training and support for locally led solutions.
  3. Promoting Local Ownership: Communities control initiatives for sustainability.
  4. Creating Collaborative Platforms: Link communities with CSOs, government, and stakeholders.
  5. Advocating for Policy Change: Influence systemic change through policy dialogues.
  6. Research: Community-based research to inform programs.
  7. Capacity Building for CBOs: Mentorship and support for grassroots organizations.

Areas of Intervention

Governance & Budget Advocacy

Transparency, public participation, and budget accountability.

We work with communities to analyze budgets, conduct social audits, and engage duty-bearers to improve governance and public resource use.

Child Protection & Safeguarding

Prevention, awareness, and stakeholder training.

Programs include caregiver training, school-based awareness, and safeguarding policy support for local institutions.

Climate & Disaster Risk

Mitigation, restoration, and resilience building.

Clean-up days, forest restoration, climate-smart agriculture, and local DRR planning.

Holistic Health

Physical, mental, and preventive health advocacy.

Screening camps, UHC advocacy, mental health awareness, and linking communities with health services.

Core Principles

  • Community-Led Approach: Communities lead decision-making and design.
  • Empowerment: Equip with knowledge, skills, and resources.
  • Collaboration: Work with stakeholders to leverage impact.
  • Equity & Inclusion: Prioritize marginalized groups.
  • Long-Term Sustainability: Build local systems for enduring change.

How We Work — The FLP Framework

FACILITATE

Enable communities to identify priorities and design solutions with our support.

LINK

Connect communities with government, NGOs, and decision-making platforms.

PARTNER

Provide capacity and resources for sustainable implementation.

Leadership Structure

The highest decision-making body is the Board (5 members) with the Executive Director as Board Secretary. Daily operations are led by the Founder/Executive Director, two Directors, thematic leads, volunteers, and interns.

We intervene directly through project implementation and indirectly through advocacy and policy engagement.
